## 1. Brand Story

The world produces billions of tons of waste every year. Yet, looking at the molecular and material level, much of this isn't truly waste—it is simply forgotten, unused, misrouted, or unmanaged. Valuable resources are discarded because there is no intelligent system to track, maintain, or coordinate their distribution.

EARTHOS AI was born from a simple belief:

> **"Nothing useful should ever become waste."**

Just as operating systems organize digital information, EARTHOS AI organizes the physical world. By giving every physical object a unique digital identity, we enable artificial intelligence to monitor its lifecycle, predict when it might become waste, and recommend the most sustainable action (repair, reuse, donation, resale, or recycling) before it is discarded.

We are transforming the global economy from a linear path (**Buy → Use → Throw Away**) into an intelligent, active circular loop (**Buy → Use → Repair → Reuse → Donate → Recycle → Rebuild**).

EARTHOS AI is not just software. It is the digital infrastructure for a sustainable future.

```
    🌍 Earth
    ♾️ Infinite Circular Economy
    🤖 Artificial Intelligence
    🌱 Sustainability
    🔄 Continuous Lifecycle

### Design Execution Principles
*   **Minimal Geometric Form:** Constructed from precise mathematical lines representing interlocking circular orbits (representing material loops) forming a subtle sphere (representing Earth).
*   **Scale Invariance:** The mark must scale cleanly from a 16x16px browser favicon or mobile app icon to a massive physical sign.
*   **Wordless Recognition:** The logo mark must be recognizable on its own without the accompanying typographic lockup.
*   **Vector Simplicity:** Designed specifically for clean vector animations (e.g., orbits rotating, paths pulsing with light, or organic expansion).

Numbers & Data: JetBrains Mono
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐

└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Hierarchy & Scale Rules
*   **H1 (Hero Headings) → 64px / Line Height: 110%** (Used for main landing pages, hero statements, and title screens).
*   **H2 (Section Headings) → 48px / Line Height: 120%** (Used for landing sections and key dashboard headers).
*   **H3 (Card Headings) → 36px / Line Height: 125%** (Used for widgets, modal titles, and action groups).
*   **H4 (Sub-headings) → 28px / Line Height: 130%** (Used for list groups and telemetry charts).
*   **Body (Paragraphs) → 18px / Line Height: 150%** (Default text size for high readability across all screen sizes).
*   **Small (Labels/Captions) → 14px / Line Height: 140%** (Used for table labels, chart legends, and metadata).

## 14. Design Principles

### I. Minimalism Over Clutter
*   Never overlay dashboards with unnecessary graphics.
*   Emphasize whitespace so that critical sustainability data points (e.g., carbon savings or chemical anomalies) draw immediate focus.

### II. Function Before Decoration
*   Every visualization, chart, and card layout must serve to communicate material flow.
*   If a visual element does not clarify an action or make resource tracking easier, it must be removed.

### III. Every Interaction Has Purpose
*   User journeys are optimized to reduce clicks.
*   Registering a material, editing a stream, or accepting a delivery must feel as simple and fast as sending a chat message.

### IV. Invisible AI Integration
*   AI recommendations should feel natural and contextual.
*   Avoid flashy "AI popups." Instead, present matches as simple, pre-validated options integrated directly into existing workflows.

### V. Mobile-First Layouts
*   Because plant operators, warehouse loaders, and individual donors use mobile devices on the go, every desktop view must scale down to a responsive mobile interface.

## 15. Visual Language

Our design system utilizes modern visual depth and organic cues to communicate the marriage of nature and technology:

*   **Organic Gradients:** We use soft, blended gradients (e.g., deep indigo transitioning into soft cyan and nature green) to replicate natural lighting (like northern auroras or sunrises).
*   **Glassmorphism:** Widgets, cards, and navigation panels feature translucent surfaces (`backdrop-filter: blur(20px)`) to keep layouts feeling open and allow background gradients to show through.
*   **Soft Shadows:** We avoid harsh, solid lines. Shadows are broad and diffuse (`box-shadow: 0px 8px 30px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.04)`) to make elements appear to float over background layers.
*   **Rounded Geometry:** Interface corners use a standard radius (e.g., `12px` for buttons, `24px` for dashboard widgets, and `32px` for parent cards) to create a soft, friendly, and human-centric environment.

## 16. Iconography Style

Icons are designed to match our focus on simplicity and precision.

*   **Consistent Stroke Width:** All system icons use a standard `2px` stroke weight.
*   **Soft Corners:** Line intersections feature slight rounding to avoid sharp, aggressive angles.
*   **Filled Active States:** Icons remain as outline representations by default, filling with primary colors only when hovered, focused, or active.
*   **Core Icon Set Examples:**
    *   *Earth:* Minimal globe icon showing coordinates.
    *   *Leaf:* Organic leaf icon representing sustainability.
    *   *QR Code:* Representing physical-to-digital scanning.
    *   *AI Spark:* A clean four-point star representing intelligent matching.
    *   *Passport:* Card icon symbolizing the Digital Product Passport.

## 17. Illustration Style

*   **Geometric 3D:** Abstract 3D representations of Earth, cities, and materials.
*   **Translucent Materials:** Illustrations utilize glass, water, and leaf textures.
*   **Dynamic Lighting:** Showcases soft directional light sources, avoiding heavy shadows and black outlines.
*   **Abstract Over Realistic:** We avoid realistic photography of garbage or scrap piles. Instead, we represent materials as colored geometric spheres or flowing streams to emphasize their value.

---

## 18. Motion Design Principles

All motion within the system must feel natural, purposeful, and reassuring:

*   **Hero Animation (800–1200ms):** A slow, continuous rotation of the 3D Earth, or the organic blending of background auroras.
*   **Standard Interface Transitions (300–500ms):** Used for switching pages, expanding modals, or updating dashboard lists. Features smooth easing (`cubic-bezier(0.25, 1, 0.5, 1)`).
*   **Micro-Interactions (150–250ms):** Quick transitions for button hovers, menu expansions, or checkmark fills.
*   **Spatial Consistency:** Items entering the screen should move logically (e.g., matched alerts slide in from the side; details scale up from the selected card).

## 19. Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.2 AA)

CIRCULARITY IS FOR EVERYONE. The design system enforces strict accessibility rules:

1.  **Color Contrast:** Text and interactive elements must maintain a minimum contrast ratio of `4.5:1` against their backgrounds (`7:1` for enhanced readability standards).
2.  **Clear Focus Indicators:** Keyboard navigators must be presented with high-contrast, animated focus borders.
3.  **Color-Independent Status Indicators:** Warning flags, error messages, and success checkmarks must pair icons or descriptive text with colors (never rely on red or green alone to convey state).
4.  **Reduced Motion Mode:** If a user configures their device for reduced motion, all transition times default to `0ms` and background auroras remain static.

## 20. Design Do’s & Don’ts

### ✅ Do
*   Keep layouts spacious, dedicating at least 25% of the screen area to clean whitespace.
*   Present environmental metrics (e.g., carbon savings, landfill diversion percentages) clearly.
*   Maintain consistent corner rounding and line weights across all custom assets.
*   Ensure that every interactive element has a clear hover and active state.

### ❌ Don't
*   Do not overlay charts or data tables with complex backgrounds.
*   Do not use auto-playing, loud, or jarring animations.
*   Do not hide essential compliance actions or reports behind deep, nested sub-menus.
*   Do not use low-contrast text colors (e.g., light gray on white) for primary labels.
